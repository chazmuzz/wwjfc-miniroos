# Volunteer Information

Volunteers help the MiniRoos programme feel organised, welcoming and manageable on busy days. Support does not need to be formal coaching to be useful.

## Where volunteers can help

- Setting up cones, goals and activity areas.
- Helping players move between stations.
- Encouraging children who need extra support or reassurance.
- Assisting coaches with simple drill flow.
- Helping pack down at the end of training.

## What good support looks like

- Keep instructions simple and positive.
- Help children stay active rather than standing around.
- Reinforce listening, effort and sportsmanship.
- Flag safety issues to a coach straight away.
- Follow the lead coach's session plan.

## Safety reminders

- Players should be wearing shin pads, boots and long socks.
- Piercings should be removed where possible, or taped safely if they cannot be removed.
- Children should be directed back to a coach if they are upset, injured or unsure.

## Why volunteer help matters

MiniRoos sessions can be busy, especially when attendance is high. Extra adult support helps keep flow, reduce delays and maintain a positive environment for all players.
