This page covers the key details for the first MiniRoos training session on Wednesday 25 March.

The main goal of the first session is to help players feel welcome, active and excited to come back the following week.

## Arrival times

- U6 players should arrive from 4:20pm for a 4:30pm start.
- U7 players should arrive from 4:50pm for a 5:00pm start.

## When you arrive

- Grab a ball and settle into simple arrival play.
- Players can kick the ball around with friends or family members while everyone gathers.
- Each age group will gather shortly after its start time, with players divided into small groups and moved to different areas.

## What to wear and bring

- Players should wear soccer boots or joggers, long socks, shin pads, and comfortable sporty clothing that does not restrict movement.
- Players should bring a drink bottle.
- Families are encouraged to bring their own size 3 ball if they have one, and to write their name on it with a Sharpie.
- The club does have footballs available, but there may not be enough to go around for every child.
- Please avoid size 4 or 5 balls.

## Team zones

For the first session, teams will begin in the following Practice Zones and then move into the game areas later in the session.

### U6

- Practice Zone 1: U6 Red, U6 Gold, U6 Pink
- Practice Zone 2: U6 White, U6 Silver
- Practice Zone 3: U6 Blue, U6 Purple

### U7

- Practice Zone 1: U7 Red, U7 Gold
- Practice Zone 2: U7 Blue, U7 Silver

For the practice part of the session, a parent or family member should accompany their child to the allocated Practice Zone and remain nearby to assist.

There will be a lot of players at the first session, so having family members close by will help the activities run smoothly.

## Session format

1. Arrival play - grab a ball and start kicking it around with a friend or family member.
2. Practice - each player has a ball and lines up on one side of the practice area. The coach will run a simple ball-mastery sequence including toe taps, side-to-side taps, running with the ball to the other side, stopping the ball on the line, and then running back to the start.
3. Practice - move into [Red Light, Green Light](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oJWGpfUMTZk) for around 5 to 10 minutes, then progress into Sharks and Minnows with the coach acting as the shark or defender.
4. Drink break.
5. Small-sided games - likely 3v3 or 4v4 depending on numbers, with no goalkeepers. Each game will run for around 8 minutes before teams rotate and continue.

## After training

- Club shorts and socks will be available for purchase from the canteen.
